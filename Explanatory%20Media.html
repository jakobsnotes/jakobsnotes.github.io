Explanatory Media

jakob13th May 2020 at 9:01am

Traditional explonatory media are

  • Books
  • Lectures

However, neither passive reading nor passive listening leads to deep understanding since Understanding requires effort. But the effectiveness of an explanatory medium depends largely how it is used. To use any explanatory medium effectively, you have to learn how to use it properly.

In general, the purpose of explanatory media is two-fold:

  1. to convey information
  2. to provide cognitive support.

One possibility to improve the situation is to utilize them in the context of well-designed Enabling Environments e.g. a great classroom. An alternative is to design new mediums that aid cognition.

Examples of alternative explanatory media

  • Textbooks that contain solved examples and exercises can trigger Metacognition.
  • Choose-your-own-adventure textbooks
  • Another example is the mnemonic medium designed by Andy Matuschak and Michael Nielsen. A proof-of-concept of this new medium is the Quantum Country website they created.
  • Explorable explanations.
  • Execute Program offers a combination of interactive lessons with interleaved live programming problems and integrates Spaced Repetition.
  • Online courses

However, it's important to keep in mind that Attempts to transform explanatory media into enabling environments are doomed. Explanatory media are traditionally transmissionist teaching devices. By turning them into enabling environments the hope is to turn them into constructivist teaching devices.

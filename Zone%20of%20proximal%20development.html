Zone of proximal development

jakob13th May 2020 at 6:25am

The zone of proximal development is an important concept related to Collaborative learning that was introduced by Russian psychologist Lev Vygotsky. The main idea is that with social support people can learn on a level just ahead of where they can manage by themselves.

More specifically, where learners can perform well with help but not alone is their zone of proximal development. (The zone therefore lies just outside his or hear Comfort zone and beyond it lies the Panic zone.) The zone also includes where the learner can be helped to go. If a learner operates in this zone, he or she continuously climbs forward, by functioning in more sophisticated ways using whatever support is available. Through this process the learner is pulled forward toward autonomous understanding and behavior at a new level.

Traditional examples for useful support are Mentors, parents, and Teachers.

The concept is useful because it makes clear that learners shouldn't simply do what they can do without help. The bar should be raised, help provided, and then the help gradually faded away until the learner operates autonomously on the new level of sophistication. A useful §Analogy is training wheels on bicycles. The technical term for the support in this context is Social scaffolding. (A scaffold in the world of builders is a temporary structure that is designed to help erecting permanent structures.)

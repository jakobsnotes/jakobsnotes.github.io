Digital Garden

jakob12th May 2020 at 1:48pm

A digital garden lives between a notebook (messy) and a blog (polished).

  • A 'seed' is planted through each backlink which creates a new empty page.
  • The garden is 'watered' with water from the 'stream' (inbox of Transient Notes, Twitter, etc.).
  • Over time, some of these seed grow into Evergreen Notes
  • After a while, it's possible to reap the fruit of one's labor which could be polished essays, research papers, videos or books.

Ideally a digital garden is publicly available because Learning in public is more effective than learning in private. A public digital garden is not only useful for the author but also for readers since it's a medium that is very different from books or blog posts. Each page in a garden represents a concept, insight, idea, or question. Since pages are densely linked, each reader can choose his own adventure. This gives the experience of wandering through the mind of another person.

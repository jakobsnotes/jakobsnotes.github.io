§Learning

jakob13th May 2020 at 8:33am

The two key ingredients for effective learning are

  • well-designed Enabling Environments
  • well-designed Explanatory Media that aid cognition

There are many principles and ideas that allow us to understand what characterizes well-designed enabling environments and learning mediums:

  • Attempts to transform explanatory media into enabling environments are doomed
  • To use any explanatory medium effectively, you have to learn how to use it properly
  • Understanding requires effort
  • Understanding requires an intense personal connection
  • Understanding requires gumption
  • Understanding requires a purpose
  • Understanding requires metacognition
  • Structured degrees should be replaced by guided learning journeys
  • People want to have their intelligence respected
  • Understanding requires multiple perspectives
  • Writing aids thinking and understanding
  • Just-in-time learning trumps just-in-case learning
  • Current educational institutions are mostly about signalling
  • The purpose of current educational institutions is filtering instead of enabling
  • Adherence is the most important factor in any system
  • The best way to learn is to teach
  • Deep insights occur without active thought
  • Consciousness is not necessary for thinking
  • Analogies are the fire and fuel of all thinking
  • The difference between a great and a mediocre thinkers has to do with the degree of wildness and craziness of the analogies that each is willing to entertain
  • The ultimate goal of learning is informed simplicity
  • Understanding is non-linear
  • Understanding requires an individual pace
  • There aren't different learning styles
  • Normal intelligence is better than high intelligence for teaching
  • Knowledge work should be carried out in sprints
  • An authored time dimension could improve explanatory media
  • There are many ways to improve how content is presented
  • Learners need guidance
  • Most of the discoveries in learning science are analogous to those in weightlifting science.

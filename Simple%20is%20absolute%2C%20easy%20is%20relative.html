Simple is absolute, easy is relative

jakob11th May 2020 at 9:14am

Simple stems from the word "sim- plex" which means one fold/braid. Hence it's an objective property and absolute. The opposite of simple is complex.

Easy stems from the words "ease < aise < adjacencs" which means "near, at hand", i.e. near to our understanding/skill set/something familiar. Hence different things can be easy for different people depending on their personal background. The opposite of easy is hard.

Not understanding the difference between simple and easy is one of the biggest problems in education. People assume that others will have no problem understanding something because it seems easy to them. But it only appears easy to them because they've seen a lot of similar things and spent a lot of time thinking about related topics. This is known as the Curse of knowledge.

Examples

  • Eating less than you burn is simple, but not easy for most people.
  • Making something people want is simple, but oftentimes not easy.
  • Telling a story about characters who grow is simple, but typically not easy.

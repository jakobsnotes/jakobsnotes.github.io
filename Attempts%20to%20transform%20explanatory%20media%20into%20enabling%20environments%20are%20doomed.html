Attempts to transform explanatory media into enabling environments are doomed

jakob11th May 2020 at 9:41am

The idea to turn Explanatory Media into Enabling Environments is very attractive because it would make them scalable.

However, attempts to transform explanatory media into an enabling environment necessarily turn them into some sort of educational game. And Educational games are not suited as enabling environments.

Examples

  • A famous example of a learning medium that is intended to be an enabling environment is The Primer that is described in the book The Diamond Age by Neal Stephenson. Although this example is often used to argue that learning mediums can be enabling environments, it's often overlooked that the Primer is not a viable enabling environment.
  • A real-world example of a learning medium that attempts to be an enabling environment (but in a more primitive way) is the Earthsearch book that was published by John Cassidy. "It's a physical multimedia books full of information, projects, and materials. It has punch-outs and thought experiments and by the time you’re done reading, the book is transformed like a Legacy game. It’s highly non-linear. Different parts appeal to different people. There’s no penalty for approaching the book as a toy box, or as an infographic, or as a narrative." - Ivan Reese
  • Explorable explanations. Interactive diagrams and small embedded sandbox simulations in may be somewhat helpful but they’re rarely useful as independent environments for the reader to think in. Instead, their main purpose is to bring a point across the author had already in mind. And Effective enabling environments serve a purpose that it intrinsically meaningful which isn't the case in such a sandbox environment.
  • Another extreme are Mathematica notebooks and other executable book. They are that environments are designed primarily for thinking about hard problems and communication is a secondary concern.

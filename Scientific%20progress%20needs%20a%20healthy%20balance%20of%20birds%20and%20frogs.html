Scientific progress needs a healthy balance of birds and frogs

jakob12th May 2020 at 2:42pm
  • Birds, “fly high in the air and survey broad vistas” (i.e. seek abstraction, unification and generalization, novel connections). Birds have “searchlight intelligence" which ranges broadly across multiple disciplines and perspectives to try to put things together.
  • Frogs, who “see only the flowers that grow nearby” (i.e. study the details of specific examples). Frogs use "laser intelligence”, which looks deep, as in fine-grained work within a discipline.

During a consolidation phase (to use the terminology introduced by Karl Popper), frogs are more important since they work out all the details of a new theory or model. But for paradigm shifts, birds are essential.

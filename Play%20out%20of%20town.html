Play out of town

jakob12th May 2020 at 4:43pm

Play out of town is one of the seven principles of effective learning in the book Making Learning Whole by David N. Perkins.

The idea is to explore different versions of and settings for the activity or topic.

Examples

  • In §Physics, classical mechanics can be described using the Newtonian Formalism, the Hamiltonian Formalism, and the Lagrangian formalism. A problem like the Harmonic oscillator can be treated in each formalism.
  • Teachers can implement the principle by providing Multi-level content.

The ideas and algorithms in mathematics are very general, but in practice students focus on a few stereotyped exercises about trains or sailboats or buying apples. The ideas about good citizenship are very general, but in practice students focus on a couple of stories about voting or community service. Even the classroom across the hall may be too far away. One of my favorite quips about learning, remembered for many years, came from a high school science teacher bemoaning his students’ troubles applying mathematics to science along these lines: “It’s as though walking across the hall from the math room to the science room, the students forget their math.” Researchers call this the problem of Transfer of learning. Playing out of town well is not something that happens automatically. Like other facets of learning, it’s something we have to work on." - Making Learning Whole by David N. Perkins.
